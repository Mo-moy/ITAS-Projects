ABOUT

The Ulaanbaatar Festival, founded in 2029, stands as a testament to the transformative power of music and community. Taking place every three years in the vibrant heart of Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia, this event is a celebration of creativity, diversity, and the timeless spirit of collaboration. From its origins, the festival has embraced a bold vision: to bring together artists and audiences from all corners of the globe to experience music in its most profound and innovative forms.

The festival’s origins are as unique as its offerings, reflecting the passion and audacity of its enigmatic founder, Jimi Khan—a figure whose love for Jimi Hendrix and global cuisine has infused every aspect of the event. Ulaanbaatar Festival merges world-class performances with a warm, welcoming atmosphere, where attendees can explore everything from traditional Mongolian sounds to cutting-edge experimental genres.

In addition to its stellar music lineup, Ulaanbaatar Festival is famed for its one-of-a-kind cheese-centric culinary experiences, featuring food trucks and stands that provide a delightful array of dairy-inspired delicacies. These offerings have become a beloved tradition, making the festival as much about flavor as it is about sound.

The upcoming 2041 edition promises to build on its legendary status, featuring performances from globally renowned artists and emerging talents alike. With mesmerizing visual art installations, eco-friendly initiatives, and an ever-expanding food scene, the Ulaanbaatar Festival continues to break boundaries and redefine what a music festival can be.

The event’s unique charm lies not just in its world-class performances but in its commitment to fostering connection. Whether you're swaying to ancient Mongolian melodies or devouring a melty grilled cheese sandwich while watching the sunset over the steppe, the Ulaanbaatar Festival offers an experience unlike any other.

Don’t miss your chance to be part of this extraordinary celebration in 2041. Join the movement, savor the magic, and let the music carry you to a world where the steppe meets the stage.
